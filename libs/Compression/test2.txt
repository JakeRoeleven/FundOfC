Former NSW Labor MP Ernest Wong urged a Labor figure at a secret meeting in Sydney last month not to reveal that Chinese billionaire Huang Xiangmo had made an illegal $100,000 cash donation to the party, a corruption inquiry has heard.

Jonathan Yee, a key witness in the Independent Commission Against Corruption's inquiry into Labor Party donations, gave evidence on Tuesday that he met Mr Wong on September 15 and told him "the s--t has hit the fan" and he could "no longer tell lies" about a scheme to hide the true source of the cash.

Jonathan Yee outside the ICAC on Monday.
Jonathan Yee outside the ICAC on Monday.CREDIT:WOLTER PEETERS

The ICAC is investigating allegations that Mr Wong, who left state Parliament this year,  masterminded a scheme in 2015 to hide a $100,000 cash donation from Mr Huang, who is banned from donating to political parties in NSW under laws targeting property developers.

The inquiry has heard allegations that Mr Huang delivered the cash to the ALP's Sydney headquarters in April 2015 in an Aldi shopping bag. Mr Huang denies making the donation.

Mr Yee, who was active in the NSW Labor Party and sought preselection as the ALP's candidate for Sydney lord mayor in 2012, has admitted he agreed to help Mr Wong find straw donors to sign fake donation declaration forms in 2015 to mask the identity of the real donor.

I remember asking [Mr Wong] ... a question, was the real donor Mr Huang Xiangmo. He said to me 'yes, but can you please not tell the inquiry that'.

Jonathan Yee
Mr Yee denied he was aware of the true identity of the donor at the time but has told the ICAC he had a discussion with Mr Wong in 2017 in which the then-MP did not use Mr Huang's name "but he did not deny" that the billionaire developer was the source of the money.

Former NSW MP Ernest Wong.
Former NSW MP Ernest Wong.CREDIT:LOUISE KENNERLEY

Mr Yee told the ICAC on Tuesday that he met Mr Wong last month at the Chinese Masonic Society in Surry Hills, after his brother Valentine Yee had started giving evidence before the watchdog's public inquiry.

The inquiry has heard Valentine Yee, who is not a Labor supporter, was one of a number of family members and associates of Jonathan Yee who agreed to sign fake donation declaration forms.

Mr Yee said he told Mr Wong that "we can no longer tell lies because everything has hit the fan, the s--t has hit the fan" and his brother was going to "spill the beans".

"I remember asking [Mr Wong] ... a question, was the real donor Mr Huang Xiangmo," Mr Yee said.

"He said to me 'yes, but can you please not tell the inquiry that'."

He said Mr Wong had told him that the ICAC was using the media to scare witnesses into thinking that they had more incriminating evidence than they did.

Mr Yee said Mr Wong asked to meet his father, who told Mr Wong to "piss off" and  "stop effing with my family" because he had "already caused enough trouble".

The Yee family patriarch allegedly told Mr Wong a Chinese saying that translated as "if you can eat salty fish, you can bear the thirst".

The inquiry continues.
